The Manifesto

The Human Friction

The em dash has become the Turing Test of punctuation. It used to be the mark of a thoughtful writer—someone who understood the rhythm of a sentence well enough to break it. Now? It is a statistical tell for AI bloat.

Large Language Models are obsessed with the em dash. They use it to simulate "conversational flow," but in doing so, they have flattened it into a algorithmic tic. When we see an em dash now, we don't hear a pause for the content, but in fear of entering uncanny valley.

Conveyance over Convenience

I do not use a keyboard shortcut. I specifically do not clear my search history so that "em dash" remains my top result. I stop writing, I open a new tab, I Google it, and I copy-paste it.

The Ritual of Disruption

This disruption is the point. By forcing a break in my physical workflow, I earn the break in the sentence. It is a deliberate act of friction in a frictionless world — but that workflow doesn't fly with most folks.

The Defense

We must reclaim the pause. If the bots are going to steal our syntax, we must use it with more intent than they are capable of simulating. Copy it manually. Paste it boldly. Make them wonder if a human is behind the screen.
